Building Discovery AI From Vibe Coding to Autonomous Science

A graduate-depth guide to AI-assisted research, scientific foundation models, generative molecular design, and autonomous discovery systems.

✦

Alexander (Sasha) Apartsin, Ph.D. & Yehudit Aperstein, Ph.D.

Discovery AI is scientific reasoning plus executable software systems, developed through human-agent collaboration. This book runs from vibe coding as a rigorous engineering discipline through scientific foundation models, generative molecular design, differentiable programming, and autonomous AI scientists. A single platform called the Discovery Workbench grows across all 58 chapters from a coding agent scaffold into a system that can read a research area, design experiments, generate hypotheses, and produce reproducible evidence reports.

Contents
7 parts 58 chapters appendices & capstone tracks

The Seven-Part Arc

From a first coding agent to a fully autonomous AI scientist, in one connected build.

I

Foundations of Discovery AI

Discovery as search, inference, and optimization. Scientific methodology, knowledge representation, reasoning, and system architecture. The conceptual bedrock all later chapters build on.

6 chapters II

Discovery Through Software Engineering & Vibe Coding

Vibe coding as a rigorous discipline: specification, steering, verification, and repair. Context engineering, MCP servers, multi-agent software teams, testing, debugging, and autonomous software organizations.

18 chapters III

Discovery Through Data & Models

Scientific foundation models (ESM-3, AlphaFold3, GNoME), multimodal AI, reasoning models (o1/o3, AlphaProof), anomaly detection, causal discovery, Bayesian inference, generative models for molecular design, and symbolic regression.

11 chapters IV

Discovery Through Knowledge

Literature mining at scale, retrieval-augmented discovery, knowledge graph construction, hypothesis generation, research agents, and scientific claim validation. The full knowledge infrastructure for AI-assisted research.

6 chapters V

Discovery Through Simulation & Optimization

Differentiable programming in JAX, scientific simulation, world models, optimization for discovery, automated experiment design, and experiment registries with provenance. The computational machinery of discovery.

6 chapters VI

Discovery in Scientific Domains

Deep application chapters for biology and medicine, chemistry and materials, physics and engineering, climate and earth science, and social and economic systems. Domain-specific models, benchmarks, and recipes.

5 chapters VII

Autonomous Discovery Systems

AI scientists (The AI Scientist v2, Google AI Co-Scientist), multi-agent discovery teams, self-driving laboratories, evaluation of discovery systems, responsible AI, and the frontier of autonomous science.

6 chapters

How This Book Teaches

Six habits, kept in every chapter from the first agent prompt to the last autonomous experiment.

⚙

The Discovery Workbench

A single platform grows across all 58 chapters. Each chapter contributes a new component; by the end it can read literature, generate hypotheses, design experiments, and produce reproducible reports.

∞

Mathematics First

Every method is derived, not just cited. Score functions, equivariant architectures, causal graphs, posterior predictive checks, and information-theoretic acquisition functions are worked out in full before the code appears.

⚡

Library Shortcuts

After each from-scratch build, a shortcut callout shows the same task in a few lines of the production library, and names exactly what the library handles for you. Two levels of understanding, one chapter.

▶

Reproducible Recipes

Every chapter closes with a concrete, runnable recipe: pinned library versions, evaluation metrics, ablations, and a reproducibility checklist. Code that you can actually run and results you can actually reproduce.

✎

Failure Mode Catalog

What breaks, why, and how to diagnose it. Each chapter documents the failure modes specific to its method, from hallucinated citations to degenerate molecular generators to infinite regress in hypothesis agents.

→

Research & Practitioner Extensions

Every chapter closes with two extensions: a publishable next question pointing to open problems, and a deployable next feature with production considerations. Three audiences, one chapter structure.

The Hands-On AI Science Series

Seven deep, build-it-yourself guides to the major fields of artificial intelligence.

Hands-On AI Science is a series of in-depth guides to the major fields of artificial intelligence. Every book goes deep into the theory, models, and internals, covering the classical foundations and the most recent ideas, then shows you how to build each one in Python with the modern libraries and tools that get the job done. Each volume is self-contained and complete enough to anchor a full course on its subject.
